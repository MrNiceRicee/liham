# Filipino Drinks

A guide to the beverages that keep the islands hydrated — from the everyday to the festive.

## Calamansi Juice

The Filipino lemonade. Smaller, rounder, and more fragrant than any lemon.

The golden ratio: $3 : 1 : 0.5$ (water : calamansi juice : simple syrup).

For a pitcher, scale linearly: $V_s = V_w / 6$

### Quick Recipe

- 12 calamansi, halved and squeezed
- 4 cups cold water
- Simple syrup to taste (start with $2/3$ cup)
- Ice — the more the better

> **Tip:** Roll each calamansi on the counter before cutting. It releases more juice.

## Sago't Gulaman

The street-side cooler. Brown sugar syrup with tapioca pearls and grass jelly — served from massive glass jars that somehow never run out.

Brown sugar syrup concentration: $250g / 500ml = 0.5$ g/ml

### Components

| Component | Prep | Texture |
|---|---|---|
| Sago (tapioca pearls) | Boil 30 min, ice bath | Chewy |
| Gulaman (grass jelly) | Set from powder, cube | Firm, slippery |
| Arnibal (brown sugar syrup) | Simmer sugar + water | Thick, caramel |
| Banana extract | 1 tsp per pitcher | Aromatic |

### Assembly

```
1. fill glass 1/4 with sago and gulaman cubes
2. pour arnibal to 1/3
3. top with cold water and ice
4. stir once — let the layers settle
```

The total sugar content per serving: $S = (V_a \times 0.5) / V_t \times 100\%$

## Tsokolate

Traditional Filipino hot chocolate — thick, dark, and frothed with a wooden *batidor*.

### Tablea (Cacao Tablets)

- Pure cacao, roasted and ground into tablets
- No sugar added — sweetness comes from you
- Dissolve in hot water or milk: $1$ tablet : $200$ml liquid

### Frothing

The *batidor* (wooden whisk) is rolled between the palms:

```
technique:
  1. hold handle between palms
  2. roll back and forth rapidly
  3. lift slightly as froth builds
  4. stop when foam holds its shape
```

The kinetic energy transfer: $E = I\omega^2 / 2$ — but honestly, just spin it fast.

> Pair with **pan de sal** fresh from the corner bakery, torn open and dunked.

## Buko Juice

Young coconut water, served straight from the shell with a straw.

- Best temperature: $4^\circ C$ (refrigerated, not iced)
- Scrape the soft meat with a spoon
- ~~Add sugar~~ — it doesn't need it

## Lambanog

Coconut wine distilled from coconut sap. Crystal clear, dangerously smooth.

**ABV:** typically $40\%$–$45\%$, though homebrew can reach $60\%$

### Flavored Variants

- Bubblegum (pink, sweet, popular at fiestas)
- Raisin-infused (darker, richer)
- Plain (the purist's choice)

> **Warning:** Lambanog hits different. Pace yourself — the coconut smoothness is deceptive.
