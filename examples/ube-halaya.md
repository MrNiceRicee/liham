# Ube Halaya

> *"Ube is not yam, and it is not taro. It is its own thing."* — every Filipino, explaining this for the hundredth time.

Purple yam jam. The base layer for everything — ice cream, pandesal filling, ensaymada topping, or eaten straight from the pot with zero regret. This recipe makes enough for a family gathering, which means you'll eat half of it before anyone arrives.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ube (purple yam) | 1 kg | Boiled, peeled, mashed |
| Coconut milk | 400 ml | Full fat, one can |
| Condensed milk | 300 ml | Sweetened |
| Evaporated milk | 150 ml | |
| Butter | 3 tbsp | Unsalted |
| Vanilla extract | 1 tsp | Optional |
| Ube extract | 1 tsp | For color, if yams are pale |

## Prep Checklist

- [ ] Boil ube until fork-tender (~30 min)
- [ ] Peel and mash while warm
- [ ] Grease the molding container with butter
- [ ] Prepare a large heavy-bottomed pot
- [x] Clear your schedule — this takes **patience**

## Steps

1. Combine mashed ube, coconut milk, and both condensed and evaporated milk in a *heavy-bottomed pot*
2. Cook over **low heat**, stirring constantly — no shortcuts here
3. Add butter and vanilla once the mixture starts to thicken
4. Keep stirring — the mixture will pull away from the sides of the pot (~45 min)
5. Transfer to a greased mold, flatten the top with a wet spoon
6. Cool completely before unmolding — overnight in the fridge is best

---

> **Lola's tip:** The stirring never stops. If your arm is tired, switch hands. If both arms are tired, you're almost done.

## Variations

### Ube Halaya Cheesecake Base

Layer cooled halaya into a graham crust and top with cream cheese mixture:

```
cream cheese    250g (softened)
heavy cream     200ml
sugar           3 tbsp
ube halaya      1 cup (spread as base layer)
```

### Quick Ube Spread

For those mornings when you want ube on pandesal but don't have 45 minutes:

- Use **ube powder** instead of fresh yams
- Microwave with condensed milk in 30-second intervals
- Stir between intervals until thick

## Storage

| Method | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Refrigerated | 5-7 days | Covered tightly |
| Frozen | 3 months | Thaw overnight in fridge |
| Room temp | Same day | Tropical heat is unforgiving |
